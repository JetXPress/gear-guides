DT 770 Headphone Compensation
All presets calibrated for DT 770 Pro 80Ω. Treble values rolled back to compensate for the 8-10kHz brightness peak. Apply Global EQ → Bright Cut 1 via Menu → Global EQ as universal correction. Cabinet models use 2x10 Redhead where possible. Reverb slightly higher than speaker use to compensate for the dry in-head feel of closed-back headphones.
Amp Only — Studio 40 effects, P&P bypassed
Pork & Pickle Only — P&P active, amp settings flat
Combined — Both dialed in together

Signal Chain
IEMs: KZ ZS10 Pro (wired) → TD-17KV phones jack · Audio: Bluetooth backing track via mobile device · Kits: Third-party rock kit backups loaded via SD card
🎧
KZ ZS10 Pro IEM Profile
V-shaped frequency response — elevated bass, recessed mids, hyped treble above 8kHz. Kick and floor tom will sound boomy without global bass correction. Snare mids will feel thin without per-pad EQ boost. Cymbals may fatigue without treble rollback. Always optimize while playing along to a Bluetooth backing track — not in silence.
Global EQ — SETUP → Bass/Treble
Bass Knob~40% (cut)
Treble Knob~45% (cut)
Bass TypeSHELV
Treble TypeSHELV

Applies across all kits. Compensates for ZS10 Pro bass bloom and 8kHz+ treble hyping. Set once, leave it.

Ambience — OTHER → AMBIENCE
TypeRoom 2 or Studio
SizeSmall to Medium
Level20–30%

Avoid Hall or Arena when playing along to Bluetooth music — creates disconnect between dry track and roomy drums in the IEM seal.

Per-Pad EQ — OTHER → PAD EQ
PadKick
ActionLow cut ~80Hz
WhyZS10 Pro bass bloom makes kick undefined
PadSnare
ActionMid boost 200–300Hz
WhyRecessed mids make snare sound thin
PadCymbals
ActionHigh cut above 8kHz
WhyZS10 Pro treble peak causes cymbal fatigue

Workflow: Load each third-party rock kit → play along to a familiar song via Bluetooth for 5 minutes → then adjust. What sounds right in silence often sounds wrong in context. The interaction between backing track and drum sound in the IEM seal is what you're optimizing for — not an isolated drum sound.
