⚠️
Auto Off — Disable This First
The TD-17KV ships with Auto Off enabled. It will cut power mid-session after inactivity. Disable it immediately: SETUP → AUTO OFF → set to OFF (or 4 HOURS minimum). Especially dangerous when you step away briefly while Bluetooth music is still playing — the module shuts down and you lose your kit settings.
KZ ZS10 Pro — IEM Optimization
🎧
KZ ZS10 Pro Frequency Profile
V-shaped response — elevated bass, recessed mids, hyped treble above 8kHz. Kick and floor tom will sound boomy without global bass correction. Snare mids will feel thin without per-pad boost. Cymbals may fatigue without treble rollback. Always optimize while playing along to a Bluetooth track — not in silence.
Global EQ — SETUP → Bass/Treble
Bass Knob~40% (cut)
Treble Knob~45% (cut)

Applies across all kits globally. Compensates for ZS10 Pro bass bloom and 8kHz+ treble peak. Set once, leave it.

Multi-Effect Quick Reference — OTHER → MULTI FX
30 types available · Applied per kit · Most useful for rock:
COMP (Compressor)Recommended
Evens out velocity differences. Essential for recording or playing with a mix. Set threshold medium, ratio medium. Start here with every new kit.
OD+DELAYSpecialty
Adds grit and room tail simultaneously. Useful for heavier rock kits that need edge without separate effects.
CHORUSModulation
Adds width and depth. Works well at low levels for a fuller sound through IEMs. Don't overdo or timing feels loose.
DELAYDelay
Adds space and sustain. Keep level low (10–15%) for rock. Too much muddies fast patterns. Better at slower tempos.
TAPE ECHODelay
Warmer than clean delay. Mimics vintage tape saturation. Good on snare for 70s-era rock room sound.
3TAP PAN DELAYDelay
Three taps panning left/right. Dramatic through IEMs — adds stereo width. More useful for practice than live feel.

For rock: Start with COMP at medium settings on every new kit before touching anything else. Most impactful single adjustment for consistency through IEMs.

Coach Mode — Practice Reference
01
TIME CHECK
Timing accuracy against the click · Press [COACH] → F1
▼
Recommended Settings
ScoreON — 8 measures
GradeEASY → HARD when consistent
Display 1SNARE
Display 2KICK
How to use it

Best used at the start of a session. Play basic kick/snare in time with the click. Screen shows BEHIND or AHEAD in real time. Target 90%+ before moving to full songs. Upgrade to HARD once you consistently hit 95% on EASY.

02
QUIET COUNT
Internal timing — click drops out mid-session · Press [COACH] → F2
▼
Recommended Settings
Measures4 measures
Quiet2 (half quiet, half click)
Why this matters

The most underused tool on the TD-17. Click plays for the first half, drops out for the second, then comes back — you find out immediately if you drifted. Playing with a bass player or backing track requires this internal clock. More valuable long-term than Time Check.

03
WARM UPS
Structured session — 3 progressive steps · Press [COACH] → F3
▼
Recommended Settings
Duration10 MINS
GradeEASY
Max Tempo180 BPM
The three steps

Step 1 — Change-Up: Note values shift every 2 measures from half notes down to sixteenths and back. Warms up limb independence.

Step 2 — Auto Up/Down: Tempo gradually increases then decreases. Exposes tempo inconsistencies without you controlling it.

Step 3 — Time Check: Final accuracy evaluation. Run this before every Bluetooth jam session.

Recording — Capture Your Practice
Record your performance to SD card as WAV — up to 60 minutes
Drums only
  1. Press [●] REC button
  2. Turn dial → select DRUMS
  3. Press [►/■] to start
  4. Press [►/■] again to stop
  5. Press [F2] PREVIEW to listen back
  6. Press [F3] EXPORT to save to SD card
Drums + Bluetooth backing track
  1. Start Bluetooth music on mobile device
  2. Press [●] REC button
  3. Turn dial → select ALL
  4. Press [►/■] to start — both record together
  5. Press [►/■] to stop
  6. Press [F3] EXPORT to save

File format: WAV, 44.1kHz, 16-bit · Saved to /REC folder on SD card · Max 60 min with SD card (3 min without). Listening back reveals timing and dynamics issues you cannot hear while playing.

Session Workflow
1. Power on → confirm Auto Off is disabled  ·  2. Connect Bluetooth  ·  3. Run Warm Ups (10 min)  ·  4. Load kit from SD card  ·  5. Set Ambience and Pad EQ while playing one familiar song  ·  6. Record a full run-through for playback review
SD Card — Quick Reference
Load a third-party kit backup
  1. Insert SD card
  2. Press [OTHER]
  3. Select SAVE/LOAD
  4. Press [F2] LOAD
  5. Select kit from SD card list
  6. Press [F3] EXECUTE → [ENTER]
Back up your customized kit
  1. Insert SD card
  2. Press [OTHER]
  3. Select SAVE/LOAD
  4. Press [F1] SAVE
  5. Select destination slot
  6. Press [F3] EXECUTE → [ENTER]

Always back up after dialing in IEM compensation and pad EQ. Third-party kits overwrite your settings when reloaded.
